# EMiF Project: Structural Change in Financial Risk (Post-COVID-19)

## Research Question
This analysis investigates fundamental shifts in financial market risk structures since the COVID-19 pandemic. Key areas of focus:
- Persistence of market volatility.
- Breakdown of traditional stock–bond diversification.
- Evolution of tail risk.

## Methodology & Pipeline
- **Data Processing:** Handling of equity, credit, commodity, and rate series. Implementation of **duration-based bond-return proxies** (US/DE 10Y) to ensure return-space comparability.
- **Diagnostics:** Jarque-Bera tests, ARCH-LM, and Ljung-Box diagnostics to validate volatility clustering.
- **Modeling Chain:**
    - GARCH/DCC (Dynamic Conditional Correlation) for multivariate volatility analysis.
    - Markov-switching models to identify regime shifts.
    - Portfolio optimization (60/40, ERC) to evaluate asset allocation stability.

## Key Findings
- Identified structural breaks in correlation regimes post-2020.
- Empirical evidence supports the transition to higher tail-risk environments in fixed-income/equity relationships.
